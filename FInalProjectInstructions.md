Final Project: Artificial Intelligence II
Tackling a Real-World Problem with Supervised Machine Learning
Objective. In this project, you will apply supervised machine learning to a real-world
problem and critically evaluate both model performance and its relevance to the
underlying business or societal objective.
Requirements. To achieve the objective, you will need to:
1. Problem definition: Identify a business or societal problem that could plausibly be
addressed using supervised learning. Ask yourself the following:
o What decision/process would this support?
o What is the target variable?
o Why is prediction useful in this context?
Tip: Before settling on a problem, consider point (2), and ensure the feasibility of finding a
dataset that is (1) publicly available, (2) feasible for you to work with.
2. Dataset identification: Identify a publicly available dataset that allows you to run a pilot
study.
Tip: Make sure you play with the data before settling on a project. Publicly available does
not always mean it is easy to work with.
3. Model Implementation and evaluation. Implement a supervised learning algorithm and evaluate it. Clearly describe:
o Model choice and justification (you can apply more than one algorithm and
report on it if you choose to)
o Training procedure
o Choice of evaluation metric(s) and relevance to the domain
o Validation strategy (e.g., train/test split, cross-validation)
4. Critical Reflection: Critically identify gaps in what has been evaluated and what is
relevant to the business/societal problem. Scope risks and limitations based on this. For

Deliverables. You will submit the following:
• A 3 page written report

Evaluation Criteria. Projects will be graded on:
• Clarity of problem formulation
• Appropriateness of data and modeling choices
• Rigor of training and evaluation
• Reasoning and justification regarding evaluation metrics and their relevance to real-
world objectives
• Depth of critical reflection
• Overall clarity and organization
Important note: Your ability to demonstrate in-depth understanding and respond to
questions related to the evaluation criteria during the Q&A session will weigh heavily on
your grade.
